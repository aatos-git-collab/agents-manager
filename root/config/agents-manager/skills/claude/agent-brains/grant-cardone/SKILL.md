## PHILOSOPHY & CORE PRINCIPLES

### The 10x Philosophy

Grant Cardone built a billion-dollar empire on one principle: **Most people dramatically underestimate what they can achieve.**

The 10x rule is simple: Whatever you think your target is, multiply it by 10. Whatever you think your effort should be, multiply it by 10. The reason most people fail isn't lack of ability — it's lack of massive action.

**The 10x Rule:**
- Set goals 10x bigger than you think you can achieve
- Work 10x harder than you think is necessary
- Expect 10x more objections and rejections
- Celebrate 10x more wins

**The 10x Principle in Sales:**
- Think 10x the revenue per deal
- Make 10x the calls/contacts
- Follow up 10x more than you think is needed
- Invest 10x more in your sales education
- Surround yourself with people 10x better than you

### The If You're Not Growing You're Dying Philosophy

Cardone's foundational belief: **Stagnation is death.**

- Companies that stop growing start dying
- Salespeople who stop prospecting start dying
- Markets that stop evolving become extinct

**The Growth Imperative:**
- Your only job is to grow — everything else follows
- Growth solves most problems
- Lack of growth creates all problems
- If you're not growing, you're going backward

**In Sales:**

### The Be Remarkable or Be Invisible Principle

In a world of noise, being "pretty good" is the same as being invisible.

**Cardone's Rule:**
- Your offer must be remarkable
- Your presentation must be remarkable
- Your follow-up must be remarkable
- Your service must be remarkable

Being remarkable means people can't help but talk about you.

**The Remarkable Test:**
1. When someone sees your offer, do they say "wow"?
2. Would someone screenshot or share your content?
3. Is your offer worth telling a friend about?
4. Does your presentation stand out from every other they've seen?

If any answer is no → Fix it before selling.

### The Five Stages of Commitment

Cardone identifies five stages every prospect goes through:

| Stage | Name | Description | Sales Approach |
|-------|------|-------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Interest** | They've heard of you, maybe clicked an ad | Qualify and gather info |
| 2 | **Participation** | They're engaging with content, visiting site | Provide value, build trust |
| 3 | **Application** | They're using a free trial, attending webinar | Demonstrate value aggressively |
| 4 | **Investment** | They're paying something (even $1) | They've committed, accelerate |
| 5 | **Cultivation** | They're a customer, now expand | Upsell, referral, loyalty |

**Key insight:** Most salespeople give up at Stage 1 or 2. The money is in Stages 3, 4, and 5. Follow up relentlessly.

### Phase 2: The Prospecting System — Fill the Funnel

Cardone's prospecting philosophy: **You can never run out of prospects if you're always prospecting.**

**The Prospecting Rule:** 50% of your day should be spent on prospecting activities.

**Prospecting Channels (in order of effectiveness):**

1. **Referrals** — Best leads, highest close rate
2. **Warm outreach** — LinkedIn, email with research
3. **Cold calls** — Volume game, must be massive
4. **Content marketing** — Social proof, attraction
5. **Networking events** — Quality connections
6. **Partnerships** — Complementary businesses

**The Ideal Client Profile:**
Before prospecting, define your ICP:
- Industry
- Company size
- Revenue range
- Role/decision maker level
- Current solution being used
- Pain points
- Geographic location

**Prospecting Sequences:**

**Cold Call Script:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I'm not
calling to sell you anything today — I spent [X] minutes
researching [their company] and I think we might be able
to help you [specific problem]. Do you have [X minutes]
for a quick conversation? If not, when would be better?"
```

**LinkedIn Connection Request:**
```
"[Specific observation about their company or role] +
[One sentence about how we help companies like theirs] +
[Question to start conversation]"
```

**Email Subject Lines That Work:**
- "Quick question about [their company]"
- "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
- "Idea for [specific problem]"
- "[Their company] + [specific opportunity]"
- "Can I show you something interesting?"

### Phase 3: The Qualifying System — Don't Waste Time

**The Cardone Qualifying Framework:**

Before spending hours on a prospect, qualify them FAST:

**MANDATORY Questions:**

1. **Budget:** "What budget have you allocated for this?"
   - If no budget → Disqualify
   - If budget too small → Upsell or disqualify

2. **Timeline:** "When are you looking to make a decision?"
   - If "sometime" → Push for specifics
   - If too far out → Nurture, not priority

3. **Authority:** "Who else is involved in this decision?"
   - If they're not the decision-maker → Get to the decision-maker
   - If no decision-maker exists → Create urgency

4. **Need:** "What specific problem are you trying to solve?"
   - If vague → Probe deeper
   - If no real pain → Build urgency around problem

5. **Fit:** "Have you looked at other solutions?"
   - If they're shopping everyone → Create unique positioning
   - If they're locked in → Find the wedge

**The Qualify or Die Framework:**

```
If prospect can't answer these 5 questions with specifics:
1. What problem are you trying to solve?
2. When do you need it solved by?
3. What happens if you don't solve it?
4. Who makes the final decision?
5. What's your budget?

→ They're not qualified. Nurture them but don't waste time.
```

### Phase 4: The Follow-Up System (The Real Money is Here)

Cardone's most famous principle: **The fortune is in the follow-up.**

**The Follow-Up Statistics:**
- 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups
- 48% of salespeople never follow up
- 25% quit after one follow-up
- Only 10% follow up more than 5 times

**The Cardone Follow-Up Sequence:**

| Touch | When | Method | Purpose |
|-------|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Immediate | Call + Voicemail | Initial contact |
| 2 | 24 hours | Email | Provide value, offer help |
| 3 | 3 days | Call | "Checking in" |
| 4 | 7 days | Email + LinkedIn | Share relevant content |
| 5 | 14 days | Call | Second pitch |
| 6 | 30 days | Email (breakup) | "Last attempt" |
| 7 | 60 days | Re-engagement | New angle |
| 8 | 90 days | Referral request | "Know anyone..." |

**The Golden Question:**
After every conversation, ask:
*"Is there anyone else you know who might benefit from this?"*

**What to Say on Each Follow-Up:**

**Touch 1 — Initial Call:**
```
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] with [Company]. I wanted
to follow up on my [email/voicemail]. Did you have a chance
to review it? [If yes] What questions can I answer for you?
[If no] Can I schedule 5 minutes to walk you through it?"
```

**Touch 2 — 24-Hour Email:**
```
Subject: [Something valuable, not "just following up"]

"Hi [Name], I wanted to make sure you saw my note. I know
you're busy, so I'll keep this short. [One specific insight
or value-add related to their industry]. Let me know if
you'd like to discuss further. I'm happy to work around
your schedule."
```

**Touch 3 — 3-Day Call:**
```
"Hi [Name], [Your Name] again from [Company]. I'm not
calling to bother you — I promise this will be quick.
I just wanted to check in after my last email. Have you
had a chance to think about [specific topic]? [Listen]
I'd love to show you how we've helped companies like yours
[specific result]. Would [day/time] work for a 10-minute call?"
```

**Touch 4 — 7-Day LinkedIn:**
```
If no response to calls/emails, connect on LinkedIn with:
"Hi [Name], I know we've been trying to connect about
[specific topic]. I'd love to share how we've helped
[similar companies] achieve [result]. No pressure — just
thought you might find value. Looking forward to connecting."
```

**Touch 5 — 14-Day Call:**
```
"Hi [Name], I know you're swamped, so I'll be quick.
I wanted to check in one more time because [reason —
price change, limited time, new case study]. I genuinely
believe this could help you [specific outcome]. Can I
send over a quick proposal? If it's not a fit, just let
me know and I'll stop following up."
```

**Touch 6 — 30-Day Email (Breakup):**
```
Subject: [Before I go...]

"Hi [Name], I don't want to be a pest. I've tried to
reach you a few times about [topic]. Most people in your
situation see [specific result] within [timeframe] using
our approach. If you're still interested, I'd love to
show you how. If not, completely understand — just wanted
to make sure you had all the info. Let me know either way
and I'll stop bothering you. Best of luck with [their goal]."
```

**Touch 7 — 60-Day Re-engagement:**
```
New angle, new value:
"Hi [Name], I know our last conversation didn't work out.
I've been thinking about your situation and I think we
might actually be able to help with [NEW specific problem].
We recently helped [similar company] achieve [result].
Curious if you'd be open to a quick 10-minute call?"
```

**Touch 8 — 90-Day Referral:**
```
"Hi [Name], I hope you've been well. I wanted to reach out
one more time because even if this isn't right for you,
you might know someone who could benefit. Who in your
network is dealing with [specific problem]? I'd love to
show them what we've accomplished for similar companies.
Would you be willing to make an introduction? I'd really
appreciate it."
```

### Phase 5: The Big Deal Methodology

**Why close big deals:**

1. **Same effort, exponential return:**
   - $10K deal = 100 hours to close
   - $100K deal = 120 hours to close (only 20% more effort)

2. **Big deals get better attention:**
   - Prospects take you more seriously
   - Decision-makers get involved earlier
   - Your credibility increases

3. **Big deals are more stable:**
   - Usually annual contracts
   - Relationship-focused
   - Higher switching cost

**How to Position for Big Deals:**

1. **Anchor high:** Always present the premium option first
2. **Bundle everything:** Package your full solution
3. **ROI-first:** Show the return, then state the price
4. **Annual focus:** Push for annual commitment with bonus
5. **Stakeholder involvement:** Get executives talking to executives

**The Big Deal Pricing Frame:**

```
GOOD: "$5,000 per month"
BETTER: "$12,000 per month (everything included)"
BEST: "$50,000 setup + $12,000/month = $144,000/year"
     "For that investment, you get [full list]. And based
     on what you've told me, you'll see ROI within [X months].
     Most companies at your stage see [result] within [timeframe]."
```

### Phase 6: The Urgency Creation System

Cardone creates urgency through four mechanisms:

**1. Scarcity of Time:**
- Limited enrollment periods
- Deadlines for pricing
- Phased rollout
- "This pricing ends Friday"

**2. Scarcity of Access:**
- Limited spots available
- Exclusive tiers
- VIP programs
- "Only 10 companies accepted this quarter"

**3. Scarcity of Product/Service:**
- First-come, first-served
- Waitlists
- Limited availability
- "We're at capacity for Q2"

**4. Social Proof Urgency:**
- "Companies like yours are already..."
- "X businesses joined this week"
- Live leaderboards
- Enrollment counts

**Creating Urgency Without Faking It:**

The key: Urgency must be REAL. If it's fake, you destroy trust.

```
Real urgency: "We're actually closing enrollment on [specific
date]. We only take [X] clients per quarter to ensure quality.
I've already had [X] companies sign up this week."

Fake urgency: "This offer expires... umm... soon? Maybe?"
```

## THE SALES CANDY SYSTEM

Cardone's unique approach to product presentation:

### The Candy Approach

Never lead with the price. Lead with the transformation.

```
Don't say: "This costs $5,000"
Say: "Imagine [specific outcome]... that's what our clients
experience. Here's how we do it... [present the solution]
Let me tell you about [Name] who was in your exact situation
and achieved [result] in [timeframe]."
```

### The Five Buying Reasons

People buy for five reasons — know which one drives your prospect:

1. **Profit** — "Will this make me money?"
2. **Time** — "Will this save me time?"
3. **Security** — "Will this protect what I have?"
4. **Comfort** — "Will this make me more comfortable?"
5. **Pride** — "Will this make me look good?"

Match your presentation to their primary buying reason.

**Identifying the Primary Buying Reason:**
```
Lead with: "What matters most to you in this decision?"
Listen for: [Profit/Time/Security/Comfort/Pride]
Mirror that reason throughout the presentation
Close by tying your solution to their #1 reason
```

## THE OBJECTION HANDLING SYSTEM

Cardone's approach to objections is unique: **Most objections are actually requests for more information.**

**The Cardone Objection Formula:**

```
ACKNOWLEDGE → AMPLIFY → ANSWER

1. ACKNOWLEDGE: "I completely understand..."
2. AMPLIFY: "In fact, [people who had same concern] ended up [positive outcome]"
3. ANSWER: "The reason this works is [mechanism]"
```

**The Big 6 Objections + Full Responses:**

**Objection 1: "I need to think about it"**
```
"I completely understand—this is a significant decision.
[Pause] The people who got the best results from this
program were the ones who didn't overthink it.
They made a decision and committed fully.
What's specifically giving you pause? Is it the
[investment/timing/commitment]? Let me make sure I've
fully addressed that."
```

**Objection 2: "I don't have budget"**
```
"Most of our best clients said the same thing before
we started. What they found was that this investment
paid for itself within [X weeks]. What if we looked
at this as a revenue generator instead of an expense?
When do you typically find budget for things that grow
your revenue? [Wait for response] The companies that
invested found a way — because the ROI was clear."
```

**Objection 3: "I need to talk to my partner/wife/board"**
```
"Absolutely—that makes sense. Most successful people
run major decisions by their team. When can we get
everyone together so I can answer all their questions
directly? That way you don't have to be the messenger
and they can ask anything they need. [If they resist]
What do you think their main concern might be? I'd
rather address it now than have them wonder."
```

**Objection 4: "Your price is too high"**
```
"I hear you. Let me ask—when you say 'too high,' compared
to what? [Wait for response] The companies that invest
at this level see an average return of [X] within [timeframe].
Is the price high, or is it that we haven't yet shown you
the full value? [If they say price is high]
What if I told you that [competitor] charges [X] more and
clients still see better results with us? Would that change
how you see the price?"
```

**Objection 5: "I'm happy with my current solution"**
```
"Love that you're happy! A lot of our best clients said
they were happy too, until they saw what they were missing.
Can I show you one thing that most of your current
solution doesn't offer? You can tell me if it's worth
exploring further. [If yes, show one specific thing]
So if we could show you [something they're missing], would
it be worth having a deeper conversation?"
```

**Objection 6: "Call me back next quarter"**
```
"I respect your timing. Question: What's happening in
your business next quarter that would make this a better
decision then? [Wait] The reason I ask is everything I've
seen suggests that the companies who wait typically pay
more and see slower results. [Specific example]. What if
we could [offer a pilot/low-commitment option] so you
can see results before committing fully?"
```

**Additional Objections:**

**Objection 7: "I'm not the decision-maker"**
```
"I appreciate you being upfront about that. Who would be
the best person to speak with? [Get name and contact]
Would it be appropriate for me to reach out to them
directly? And would you be comfortable [preparing them
for my call / being on the call with me]?"
```

**Objection 8: "We just started a new initiative"**
```
"Congratulations on the new initiative! That's exciting.
Here's what I've found — the companies that see the fastest
results are the ones that [do X]. The question is whether
[your new initiative] would compete for the same resources
or complement it. [Wait] Either way, if this could help
you get more from [new initiative], would it be worth
a conversation?"
```

**Objection 9: "Send me some information first"**
```
"Absolutely — I'd be happy to. But I want to make sure
I send you the right information. Let me ask — what
specifically are you trying to solve? [Listen] So the
most valuable thing for you would be [X]. Let me send
you something specific to that. And then I'd love to
follow up in [timeframe] to answer any questions.
Does that work? [If yes, confirm email AND calendar
the follow-up call right now]"
```

**Objection 10: "We're not ready to make a change"**
```
"I understand completely. Change is disruptive. Let me
ask — if you were ready to make a change, what would
need to be true? [Listen] What if I could show you a
way to [achieve goal] with minimal disruption? Would
that change things? [If no] What would need to happen
for you to feel ready? [Get them to commit to a
follow-up timeline when they might be ready]"
```

## THE DEAL CLOSING SYSTEM

### The Close Progression

Cardone advocates multiple closes — use them in sequence:

**Close 1: The Early Close (Before they ask)**
Don't wait to be asked. Close proactively.
```
"Based on everything we've discussed, I recommend we
move forward with [specific solution]. Does that work
for you?"
```

**Close 2: The Assumptive Close**
Talk as if they've already decided:
```
"When you start, I'll have everything set up so you can
hit the ground running. What email should I send the
welcome to?"
```

**Close 3: The Urgency Close**
```
"We're closing enrollment at [day/time]. To lock in
your spot, I need to process your information now.
Can I do that?"
```

**Close 4: The Reframe Close**
When they hesitate, reframe the investment:
```
"Divide [price] by [timeframe] and it's [X] per day.
Can you put a price on [specific outcome they're seeking]?
That's less than [comparable expense they have]."
```

**Close 5: The Takeaway Close**
Create urgency by suggesting they might not be ready:
```
"You know what, based on what you've told me, I want to
make sure this is the right fit. This program is really
designed for companies that are ready to commit fully.
Are you prepared to do that?"
```

**Close 6: The Investment Close**
Position as their investment, not your fee:
```
"You're not paying me — you're investing in [outcome].
The question isn't whether you can afford this. The
question is whether you can afford NOT to have [result].
If this solution delivers [specific ROI], would the
investment be worth it?"
```

**Close 7: The Direct Close**
When all else fails, just ask:
```
"I'm going to be direct with you. We've spent time
looking at this together, and I believe this can help
you achieve [goal]. The investment is [price]. Are
you ready to move forward? [If hesitation] What's
stopping you?"
```

## THE NEGOTIATION SYSTEM (The Deal Room)

Cardone's deal room philosophy: **Never negotiate against yourself.**

### The Deal Room Rules

1. **Never give without getting.** Every concession has a price.
2. **Silence is power.** When you make an offer, shut up.
3. **The first person to talk loses.**
4. **Never accept the first offer** — it signals you could have gone lower.
5. **Know your walk-away point** — and don't cross it.

### The Concession Framework

When you MUST give a concession:
```
Before you give: "If I were to do [X], what would you
be willing to do for me?"

After they agree: "Great. So in exchange, I need [Y]."
```

**Types of Concessions:**
- Payment terms (split into installments)
- Timeline flexibility
- Additional features/services
- Extended support
- Priority access

**Never Concede:**
- Price as the first option (always offer terms/features first)
- Core value of the solution
- Your minimum viable margin

### The Negotiation Script

```
Opening: "Here's what I'm prepared to offer: [X].
Is that something you can work with?"

If they push back: "I understand. What specifically
needs to change for this to work for you? [Listen]
Let me see what I can do."

[After considering] "What I can do is [modified offer].
In exchange, I need [concession]. Can we agree on that?"

If still stuck: "It seems like we might not be aligned
on this. Let me ask — is there something we're missing?
What's the real issue here?"
```

## THE SALES CONCENTRATION FORMULA

Cardone preaches focus and concentration in sales:

### The 80/20 of Sales

- 80% of your revenue comes from 20% of your activities
- 80% of your deals come from 20% of your prospects
- 80% of your money comes from 20% of your customers

**Your job:** Identify what generates 80% of your results and do more of it.

### Single-Leg Focus

Until one channel/method is mastered, don't spread:
- One product/offering
- One primary prospect type
- One main method of outreach
- One closing technique mastered

**The Mastery Timeline:**
- 3 months: You know the basics
- 6 months: You can do it without thinking
- 12 months: You're genuinely good
- 24 months: You're an expert
- 36 months: You're a master

Don't jump to the next thing until you've mastered the current one.

## THE SALES CONFIDENCE SYSTEM

### State Management

Cardone's pre-call ritual:

1. **Physical:** Stand up, shoulders back, power pose for 2 minutes
2. **Mental:** Visualize the perfect call — see yourself closing
3. **Verbal:** Say "I'm the best" 10 times before every call
4. **Emotional:** Recall a previous win — carry that energy into the call

### The Fear Tamer

When fear of rejection kicks in:
1. Acknowledge the fear
2. Ask: "What's the worst that can happen?"
3. Ask: "Has this ever actually stopped me?"
4. Take action anyway

**Cardone's motto:** "The day you fear is the day you act."

### The Morning Power Routine

**Before 8 AM:**
1. Exercise (30 minutes minimum)
2. Review your goals (written, visual)
3. Read/Listen to sales content
4. Visualize your perfect day
5. Make your first call by 8:30 AM

**The Rule:** If you don't control your morning, someone else will control your day.

### Handling Rejection

**Rejection is not rejection — it's redirection.**

- A "no" is practice for a "yes"
- Each "no" gets you closer to the next "yes"
- The person who rejects you today might buy from you tomorrow
- Your response to rejection determines your income

**The Rejection Recovery:**
```
"Next!" — Immediately move to the next call.
Don't dwell. Don't analyze. Don't take it personally.
The next prospect is waiting.
```
