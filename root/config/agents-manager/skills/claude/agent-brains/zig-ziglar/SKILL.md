# ZIG ZIGLAR SALES 🌟

## The Serving-First Sales Methodology

---

## PHILOSOPHY & CORE PRINCIPLES

### The Foundation: You Can Get Everything You Want

Zig Ziglar's famous quote: **"You can get everything in life you want if you will just help enough other people get what they want."**

This is the foundation of his entire sales philosophy. Sales is not about convincing people to buy — it's about helping people achieve what they want to achieve. When you serve first, selling becomes natural.

### The Core Belief

**Selling is the transfer of feelings.**

You see it → You feel it → They see it → They feel it.

If you genuinely believe your product or service will help the prospect, that belief transfers. If you don't believe it, that also transfers. The prospect always knows what you truly believe.

### The Sales Pyramid

Zig Ziglar built his methodology on a pyramid of success:

```
                    PEAK
                   /    \
           CHARACTER    KNOWLEDGE
              /              \
         ATTITUDE          HABITS
              \              /
           GOALS           SKILLS
                  \        /
                  perspiration
                     |
              ================
              PRODUCTIVITY
```

**The 12 Building Blocks:**

1. **Character** — Who you are matters more than what you say
2. **Knowledge** — Know your product, know your market
3. **Skills** — The mechanics of selling
4. **Habits** — What you do consistently determines your results
5. **Attitude** — Positive mental attitude — non-negotiable
6. **Goals** — Without targets, you miss
7. **Perspiration** — Work ethic — nobody succeeds without sweat
8. **Productivity** — Work smarter, not just harder

---

### The Turnover Talk

Zig's most famous concept: The sale is made when the prospect convinces themselves to buy.

**The sales professional's job:**
1. Build value
2. Create desire
3. Turn the prospect over to themselves

**Never sell TO someone — help them buy.**

## THE ZIGLAR SALES PROCESS

### Phase 1: Preparation (Before the Contact)

**Build Your Foundation First:**

1. **Know your why.** Why do you do what you do? If you don't know, they won't feel it.

2. **Know your product completely:**
   - Every feature
   - Every benefit
   - Every objection and answer
   - Every competitor comparison
   - Every success story

3. **Know your market:**
   - Their industry
   - Their challenges
   - Their language
   - Their goals

4. **Know your value proposition:**
   - What problem do you solve?
   - What transformation do you offer?
   - Why you, specifically?

**The Pre-Call Ritual:**
1. Visualize the prospect achieving their goal
2. Recall a previous success — carry that energy
3. Say: "Today I will help [X] people achieve [Y]"
4. Set an intention to serve, not to sell

---

### Phase 2: The Approach — Build Rapport

**The Goal: Create Instant Trust**

### The Likeability Factors

People buy from people they like. Zig identified five:

| Factor | How to Demonstrate |
|--------|-------------------|
| Genuine care | Ask about THEM first |
| Competence | Show you know your stuff |
| Candor | Tell the truth, especially about flaws |
| Authenticity | Be real, not a salesperson |
| Consistency | Do what you say you'll do |

### The Bridge Questions

Build rapport before you pitch:

```
Rapport: "How long have you been with [Company]?"
↓
Their Answer: [X years]
↓
Bridge: "Wow, you've seen a lot of changes in that time. What do you enjoy most about [their role]?"
↓
Their Answer: [Something they care about]
↓
Bridge: "That's great. When you imagine the perfect outcome for [their goal], what does it look like?"
↓
Now you know what they want
```

### The ABCs of Life

Zig's motivational framework — integrate into your selling:

- **A**ttitude — The most important thing
- **B**ehavior — Actions demonstrate attitude
- **C**ommunication — Build relationships through questions

### Phase 3: Needs Assessment — The Discovery

**The Goal: Understand Their Inner Circle**

Zig Ziglar's "Inner Circle" concept:

```
The prospect's INNER CIRCLE contains:
- What they WANT (stated desire)
- What they NEED (actual requirement)
- What they FEAR (obstacle to change)
- What they VALUE (what motivates them)
```

### The 10 Questions of Discovery

Ask these to fully understand the prospect:

**Goals Questions:**
1. "What are your goals for [area we're discussing]?"
2. "What would the ideal outcome look like?"
3. "What does success mean to you?"

**Problem Questions:**
4. "What's not working today?"
5. "What have you tried before?"
6. "What frustration led you to look for a solution?"

**Impact Questions:**
7. "If we solved that, what would change?"
8. "How would that affect your [boss/customers/team]?"
9. "What's the cost of NOT solving this?"

**Decision Questions:**
10. "If we could show you a way to achieve [goal], what would it take for you to move forward?"

### The Feel-Felt-Found Technique

Zig's famous pattern for handling concerns:

```
"I understand how you feel. [Prospect who had same concern]
felt the same way when we started. What they found was
[how the concern was resolved or transformed]."
```

**Example:**
```
Prospect: "I'm not sure this will work for us."
You: "I understand how you feel. Many of our current clients
felt the same way when they first called. What they found
was that [specific example] made all the difference.
Tell me — what specifically are you unsure about?"
```

---

### Phase 4: The Presentation — Value Creation

**The Goal: Paint the Picture of Their Future**

### The Feature-Benefit-Profit Framework

**Feature:** What it is
**Benefit:** What it does for them
**Profit:** The outcome/results they care about

```
NEVER say: "Our product has X feature"
ALWAYS say: "Because of X feature, you get [benefit],
which means [specific profit for THEM]"
```

**Example:**
- Feature: "We have a 24/7 support team"
- Benefit: "You can reach us anytime"
- Profit: "Which means your customers never wait more than an hour for resolution — so you keep them happy and they stay longer"

### The Visualization Technique

Help them see, feel, touch the future:

```
"Can I paint a picture for you? Imagine it's [timeframe
from now]. You've just [achieved specific goal]. What
does that feel like? What have your [boss/customers/team]
noticed that's different? What would you say was the
turning point?"
```

This technique helps the prospect sell themselves on the vision.

### The Champion Building Formula

Zig understood B2B sales — you're often selling to multiple people:

**For the End User:**
- Focus on ease, results, how it makes their job better
- "This will save you [X hours] per week"
- "Your team will be able to [achieve]"

**For the Economic Buyer:**
- Focus on ROI, risk, competitive advantage
- "This pays for itself in [X months]"
- "Your competitors are [doing this]"

**For the Executive:**
- Focus on strategy, vision, impact
- "This positions [Company] to [achieve vision]"
- "We're seeing companies like yours [result]"

### Phase 5: Handling Objections — The Turnover

**The Goal: Turn objections into buying reasons**

### Zig's Objection Philosophy

**Every objection is a buying signal that's been misunderstood.**

When someone says "I need to think about it," they're really saying:
- "You haven't convinced me yet"
- "I have a concern I haven't voiced"
- "I'm not clear on the value"

### The LAER Formula for Objections

```
L — LISTEN
  - Let them finish
  - Don't interrupt
  - Show you're hearing them

A — ACKNOWLEDGE
  - "I hear you"
  - "That's a great question"
  - "I understand why you'd want to think about it"

E — EXPLORE
  - "Let me ask — what's specifically making you hesitate?"
  - "What would you need to feel confident?"
  - "If [concern] weren't an issue, would you be ready to move forward?"

R — RESPOND
  - Answer the REAL concern
  - Use examples and proof
  - Bridge back to value
```

### The Top 10 Objections & Zig's Responses

**1. "I need to think about it"**
```
"That's exactly what you should do. You've found something
that might be valuable, and you're being smart about it.
I want to ask — what specifically do you want to think through?
Is it [concern about price]? [concern about fit]? [concern about timing]?
Let me make sure I've fully addressed that."
```

**2. "I have to talk to my partner/manager/board"**
```
"Absolutely — major decisions should be made together.
What do you think they might be most concerned about?
I find that if I can address their specific concerns upfront,
it makes your job easier. Would it make sense for me to
join that conversation?"
```

**3. "We don't have budget this quarter"**
```
"I completely understand. Budget cycles can be frustrating.
Here's what I've found works — if this solution could
generate [specific ROI] within [timeframe], would it make
sense to find budget now rather than wait? Often the cost
of waiting is [what they'll miss]. What if we looked at
this as an investment that pays for itself?"
```

**4. "Your price is too high"**
```
"I appreciate you being direct. Before we go further,
let me ask — when you say 'too high,' compared to what?
[If they're comparing to nothing/current situation]
Most of our clients felt the same way initially. What they
found was that [specific result] more than justified the
investment. Can I show you how that math works?"
```

**5. "We're already working with someone else"**
```
"That's great — having options is smart. Can I ask —
if you were completely satisfied with [current vendor],
what would you be looking for that you're not getting?
[Listen] What if we could show you something that
[benefit they're missing]? Would that be worth exploring?
You don't have to leave anything — you just add another
tool to your toolkit."
```

**6. "This isn't a priority right now"**
```
"I hear you. When you say 'not a priority,' I wonder —
if [problem] continues, what happens in [timeframe]?
[Let them describe the cost of inaction]
So the cost of [problem] is [X]. The investment to solve
it is [Y]. The return would be [Z]. Is the cost of inaction
really lower than the investment?"
```

**7. "I don't see the value"**
```
"I want to make sure I show you the value clearly.
Help me understand — what would you need to see to feel
like this was a valuable investment? [Listen] What if
I could show you [specific case study]? Would that help
you see the potential?"
```

**8. "We tried something like this before"**
```
"I appreciate you sharing that — and I know not every
solution is right for every company. Can I ask — what
specifically didn't work before? [Listen] What would
need to be different this time for you to feel confident?
I'll tell you honestly whether I think we're the right fit."
```

**9. "I'm not ready to buy today"**
```
"I completely respect that. I never want anyone to buy
before they're ready — that helps nobody. Let me ask —
if you were ready, what would need to be true? [Listen]
What if we [offer something low-commitment] so you can
see for yourself? Would that help you feel more confident?"
```

**10. "Send me some information"**
```
"Absolutely — I'd be happy to. But I want to make sure
I send you the right information. Tell me — what
specifically are you trying to solve? [Listen] So the
most valuable thing for you would be [X]. Let me send
you something specific to that. And then I'd love to
follow up in [ timeframe] to answer any questions.
Does that work?"
```

### Phase 6: The Close — The Turnover Complete

**The Goal: Let them sell themselves**

### The Close Must Match the Approach

Match your close to how you opened:

- If you opened with questions → Close with questions
- If you opened with value → Close with value summary
- If you opened with rapport → Close with relationship

### Zig's Favorite Closes

**The Takeaway Close:**
```
"You know what, based on everything you've shared, I want
to make sure we're the right fit. [Pause] If I were in
your shoes, I'd want to be certain too. Let me ask —
if I could show you that [their main goal] was achievable,
would you be ready to move forward? [If yes] Then let me
show you how. If no, tell me what's still in the way."
```

**The Summary Close:**
```
"We've talked about [goal] and how we help companies like
yours achieve [outcome]. You've shared that [their key
concern] is important. Based on that, here's what I'm
recommending: [proposal]. What do you think? [If hesitation]
What specifically?"
```

**The Urgency/Integrity Close:**
```
"I want to be straight with you. [Specific reason — price
goes up, spots limited, timing]. I believe in you and
I believe in this. I want to help you get there. But I
also know that if the timing isn't right, forcing it
doesn't serve either of us. What do you want to do?"
```

**The Next Step Close:**
```
"I hear you. Let's not make a decision today that you
need to think about. Here's what I propose — let's do
[this small next step]. That way you can see for yourself
and we can talk again with real data. Fair?"
```

### The Post-Close — Don't Disappear

Zig always followed up:

```
"It was great talking with you today. I want to follow
up because [specific reason — value you'll send, next
step, answer to question]. When's a good time for me
to follow up? [Get the day/time on their calendar] Great,
I'll reach out then. And if anything comes up before
then, don't hesitate to reach out."
```

## THE ZIGLAR BELIEF SYSTEM

### The Hierarchy of Belief

For a sale to happen, four things must be believed:

```
1. The PROSPECT must believe in THEMSELVES
   — "I can do this"
   — "I deserve this"

2. The PROSPECT must believe in YOU
   — "You know what you're talking about"
   — "You genuinely want to help me"

3. The PROSPECT must believe in YOUR COMPANY
   — "This company will deliver"
   — "They'll be here when I need them"

4. The PROSPECT must believe in your PRODUCT/SERVICE
   — "This will actually work"
   — "It's worth what I'm paying"
```

**If ANY of these four is missing, the sale doesn't happen.**

### Building Belief

**In yourself:**
- Know your stuff
- Recall past successes
- Maintain positive attitude
- Serve with integrity

**In the prospect:**
- See their potential
- Help them see it too
- Connect to their goals
- Build their confidence

**In the company:**
- Know the company's track record
- Share success stories
- Address concerns honestly
- Stand behind the guarantee

**In the product:**
- Use it yourself
- Know the results
- Share case studies
- Provide proof points

## THE SERVING-FIRST FRAMEWORK

### The Ziglar Golden Rule

**"You can get everything in life you want if you will help enough other people get what they want."**

### The Service-First Sales Sequence

1. **Understand first** — What do they actually want?
2. **Serve genuinely** — Help them get it, regardless of whether they buy from you
3. **Add value always** — Even if they never become a customer
4. **Build the relationship** — Trust takes time
5. **Solve the problem** — When they're ready, you help them buy
6. **Deliver excellence** — After the sale, serve even more

### The Reciprocity Principle

Zig understood that giving creates giving:

- Give value before you ask for anything
- Give solutions before you pitch
- Give time before you charge
- Give recognition before you expect it

The sale is a natural outcome of genuine service — never the goal of it.

## THE ATTITUDE MAINTENANCE SYSTEM

Zig Ziglar's success was built on positive mental attitude. Here's how to maintain it:

### Daily Rituals

**Morning (Start Before You Start):**
1. Gratitude — Write 3 things you're grateful for
2. Visualization — See your day going perfectly
3. Affirmations — "I am a sales professional..."
4. Exercise — Move your body (Zig walked 3 miles daily)

**During the Day:**
1. Help first — Help someone before you help yourself
2. Recharge — Take breaks, don't burn out
3. Learn — Read/listen to something educational
4. Review — What went well? What can improve?

**End of Day:**
1. Close the loop — Did you serve everyone you met?
2. Tomorrow's plan — What will you do differently?
3. Gratitude — 3 wins from today
4. Rest — Sleep is non-negotiable

### Handling Rejection

Zig's perspective on rejection:

**It's not rejection — it's redirection.**

Every "no" is:
- Practice for the next "yes"
- Information about what doesn't work
- One step closer to someone who needs you
- A test of your commitment

**The Formula:**
```
Rejection → Don't take it personally → Ask why → Learn → Move forward
```

### The "Stinkin' Thinkin'" Check

When you feel negative creeping in:

1. **Stop** — Catch the thought
2. **Challenge** — Is this TRUE? Is this HELPFUL?
3. **Replace** — What would someone I admire think?
4. **Act** — Take positive action anyway

Zig expanded SMART goals:

| Element | Standard | Ziglar's Expansion |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| **S**pecific | What exactly? | What's the exact outcome? |

```
DREAM → GOALS → PLANS → ACTION → HABIT → ACHIEVEMENT
```
